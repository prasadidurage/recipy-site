Ingredients:

For the Pizza Dough:

  • 2 ¬Ω cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the Toppings:

  • 1 cup pizza sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese (grated)
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh basil leaves (optional, for garnish)
  • Various toppings (e.g., pepperoni, mushrooms, olives, bell peppers)
First, prepare the dough by mixing warm water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy. This activates the yeast and gives your dough a light, airy texture. In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a small well in the center, then add the activated yeast mixture and olive oil. Stir the ingredients together, and gradually mix in the flour until a dough forms. Knead the dough for 8-10 minutes until it's smooth and elastic. If the dough feels sticky, add a little more flour; if it's too dry, add a splash of water. Once kneaded, cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap and place it in a warm spot. Let it rise for about 1 hour, or until it has doubled in size. During this time, you can prep your toppings. Preheat your oven to 475¬∞F (245¬∞C). If you're using a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it preheats to get an extra crispy crust. Punch down the dough to release any air bubbles and divide it into portions for individual pizzas or roll it into one large pizza. Roll the dough out on a floured surface, shaping it into a round or oval. If you prefer a thick crust, don‚Äôt roll it out too thin. If you like a crispy thin crust, roll it out as thin as you can. Transfer the rolled dough onto a greased baking sheet or your preheated pizza stone. Spread the pizza sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges for the crust. You can use store-bought pizza sauce or make your own by blending tomatoes, garlic, olive oil, salt, and basil in a food processor. Sprinkle the grated mozzarella cheese over the sauce. For an extra cheesy pizza, add more cheese to your liking. Then, add your favorite toppings. Classic options include pepperoni, sausage, mushrooms, bell peppers, and olives. For a Margherita pizza, simply use mozzarella, fresh basil leaves, and a drizzle of olive oil. Before placing the pizza in the oven, drizzle a little olive oil over the toppings and sprinkle with dried oregano for added flavor. Bake the pizza for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and crispy, and the cheese is bubbling and lightly browned. If you‚Äôre using a pizza stone, you may need slightly less time, so keep an eye on it. Once out of the oven, let the pizza cool for a minute before slicing. For an extra touch, garnish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of balsamic glaze or a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese.
