First, prepare the dough by mixing warm water, sugar, and yeast in a small bowl. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, baking powder, and baking soda. Add yogurt, warm milk, oil, and the yeast mixture, then knead into a soft, slightly sticky dough. Cover and let it rest in a warm place for 1-2 hours until it doubles in size. Once the dough has risen, divide it into equal portions and roll each into an oval or round shape. Heat a tawa (griddle) or a cast-iron pan over medium-high heat. Place a rolled-out naan on the hot pan and cook for about 1 minute until bubbles form. Flip and cook the other side until golden brown spots appear. If desired, place the naan directly over the flame for a few seconds to get a tandoor-like effect. Brush the hot naan with melted butter and sprinkle with chopped coriander. For garlic naan, mix minced garlic with melted butter before brushing.
