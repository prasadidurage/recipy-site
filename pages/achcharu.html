Ingredients:

Main Ingredients

  • 200g carrots (julienned)
  • 200g green papaya (julienned)
  • 100g shallots (peeled and halved)
  • 3-4 green chilies (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (sliced)
  • 1-inch ginger (sliced)

For Pickling:

  • Â½ cup white vinegar
  • 1 tbsp mustard seeds (ground into a paste)
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)

For Tempering:

  • 2 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
First, prepare the vegetables by slicing carrots, papaya, green chilies, ginger, and garlic into thin strips. Peel and halve the shallots. In a small bowl, mix mustard paste with vinegar, sugar, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Heat coconut oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter. Then, add curry leaves and mix well. Pour this tempering over the prepared vegetables and mix thoroughly. Add the mustard-vinegar mixture and toss everything together, ensuring the vegetables are well coated. Let the achcharu sit for at least a few hours to allow the flavors to blend. For best results, leave it overnight for a richer taste. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for longer shelf life. Achcharu is a popular Sri Lankan pickle often enjoyed as a side dish with rice and curry. It adds a tangy, spicy, and slightly sweet touch to meals, making it a favorite in Sri Lankan cuisine. Some variations include adding pineapple, mango, or even dates for a unique twist. The preparation of Achcharu has been a long-standing tradition in Sri Lankan households, often made in large batches for festive occasions and celebrations. It is a versatile condiment that pairs well with not only rice but also flatbreads and snacks. Many families have their own special Achcharu recipes, passed down through generations, each adding a distinctive twist by incorporating local fruits and vegetables. Achcharu is best served fresh but also develops a deeper flavor profile over time. If you prefer a milder taste, adjust the chili and mustard levels accordingly. To enhance the flavor even more, some people add a dash of roasted curry powder or crushed dried chilies for an extra kick.
